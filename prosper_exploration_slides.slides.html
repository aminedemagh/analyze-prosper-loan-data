Prosper Loan Data Exploration¶

by Demagh Mohamed El Amine¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this report, I wanted to investigate the features that could best predict the outcome of a loan (completed or charged-off). I explored the 5 main variables of a Prosper loan: loan amount, listing category, loan term, lender yield, and Prosper rating.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset contained information about 113937 loans and 81 variables. It included two periods of the company: a pre-2009 period before the enactment of the laws for online lending and a post-2009 period where Prosper had changed its business model to comply with the new laws. I only looked at the loans that originated after 2009 because I wanted to capture the latest trends in the data. My subset consisted of 84997 loans that originated from 6 August 2009 to 27 March 2014.

Loan Status¶

There are 11 values for LoanStatus but this analysis will mostly focus on the outcome of a loan (Current or Completed).

Of the loans with an outcome, there were 78.65% completed loans and 21.35% charged-off loans. This means that over 5 years, 1/5 of the loans were charged off.

Prosper Rating vs. Loan Status¶

Prosper rating is a proprietary system used to assess the estimated loss rates associated with a specific loan. It is based on the borrower's personal information. As we can see below, the percentage of charged-off loans increases with the increase of Prosper rating.

The question that arises is: can Prosper Rating explain all the variation in the count/percentage of charged-off loans or do the other variables have an independent effect on the loan outcome?

To answer this question, I will plot each variable with both the loan outcome and Prosper rating.

Prosper Rating vs. Listing Category vs. Loan Status¶

There are 19 listing categories but I will focus on the 9 most common ones.

The percentage of charge-off loans increases for each listing category with the increase of Prosper rating. However, the ranking of the categories is different from a prosper rating to another. For example: 'Large Purchases' category had the least percentage of chargeoffs in AA but became the second in E.

Propser Rating vs. Lender Yield vs. Loan Status¶

Note : The lender yield is the annual interest that an investor takes.

The distribution of lender yield for both completed and charged-off loans is shifted upwards with the increase of Prosper rating.

From AA to C, the distribution of charged-off loans has higher lender yield values. The Q1 of the charged-off distribution is higher or equal to the median of the distribution of completed loans.

From D to HR, the difference between the distribution of lender yield for charged-off and completed loans is weaker. The median lender yield for charged-off loans is still higher but the Q1 is lower than the median lender yield for completed loans.
